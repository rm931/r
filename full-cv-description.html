PhD position

Nature of Duties

My PhD research centered on assessing the cost-benefit impacts of genetically modified crops on agro-ecosystems, particularly within the context of European Union (EU) policies such as Directive 2015/412. This directive emphasizes sustainable agricultural practices, biodiversity conservation, and reducing dependency on pesticides. To support these objectives, I developed a spatially explicit bioeconomic modeling framework. This innovative model employed advanced tools, including R, Python, and QGIS, to evaluate how invasive pest outbreaks impact crop productivity, farm economics, pesticide usage, and biodiversity in the Aragon region of Spain. By integrating ecological dynamics with farmer decision-making, my research identified strategies to optimize land use, reduce pesticide reliance, and enhance ecosystem services, contributing to sustainable agricultural practices.

The findings of my research demonstrated that while Bt maize effectively reduces pesticide use, it is not a standalone solution. Effective integrated pest management (IPM) and predictive modeling are essential to mitigate the risks of pest resurgence, including secondary and invasive pest outbreaks, and to maintain the balance between agricultural productivity and biodiversity conservation. These conclusions underscore the critical role of adaptive pest management strategies in promoting sustainable agriculture within policy frameworks.

My PhD was conducted as part of the FP7 AMIGA project, a major EU-funded initiative designed to assess and monitor the environmental and economic impacts of genetically modified plants (GMPs) in European agro-ecosystems. This multidisciplinary project brought together 22 partners, including EFSA and leading European institutions, across 15 EU countries and Argentina. Through this collaboration, I contributed to harmonizing metrics for environmental risk assessment (ERA) and post-market monitoring of GMPs. These partnerships significantly enhanced my ability to integrate economic, ecological, and policy perspectives, ultimately producing actionable recommendations for decision-makers at the EU level.

The bioeconomic modeling framework I developed was pivotal to my research.
